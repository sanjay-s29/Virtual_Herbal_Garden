Jackfruit Leaves
Botanical Name: Artocarpus heterophyllus
Common Names: Jackfruit, Kathal (Hindi), Jakfruit (Malay), Nangka (Indonesian)
Family: Moraceae
Description: Jackfruit leaves come from the large, tropical tree known for its massive fruit. These leaves are glossy, dark green, and large, and have a wide range of uses in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
Medicinal Uses:
  • Anti-inflammatory: Helps reduce inflammation in the body.
  • Blood Sugar Regulation: Traditionally used to regulate blood sugar levels, especially for diabetics.
  • Antioxidant Properties: Rich in antioxidants, it helps neutralize free radicals.
  • Digestive Health: Jackfruit leaves have been used to treat digestive problems and improve gut health.
  • Anti-cancer: Contains compounds that are believed to have anti-cancer properties.
Culinary Uses:
  • Leaf Wrapping: Jackfruit leaves are commonly used to wrap food, especially in Southeast Asian cooking, to infuse flavor during cooking.
  • Herbal Tea: The leaves are sometimes boiled to make a tea with medicinal properties.
  • Used in Cooking: Fresh jackfruit leaves are sometimes added to curries and other dishes for their unique flavor and health benefits.
Places Where Found:

Jackfruit trees are native to the rainforests of Southwest India, and are now widely cultivated in tropical regions across Asia, Africa, and South America.

Cultural and Historical Significance:

In many tropical regions, jackfruit is a staple food. The tree and its leaves are revered in several cultures for their uses in food preparation, traditional medicine, and religious rituals.

Chemical Composition:
  • Flavonoids: Known for their anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties.
  • Tannins: Used for their astringent properties and their ability to help with gastrointestinal issues.
  • Polyphenols: Help with oxidative stress and are beneficial for cardiovascular health.
How to Use:
  • Leaf Tea: Steep fresh or dried leaves in hot water to make a medicinal herbal tea.
  • Leaf Powder: The leaves can be dried and powdered to be added to foods or used in health supplements.
  • Wraps: Use fresh jackfruit leaves to wrap food during cooking to impart a subtle flavor.
