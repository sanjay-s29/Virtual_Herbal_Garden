Karuka Leaves
Botanical Name: Carica papaya
Common Names: Karuka, Papaya Leaves
Family: Caricaceae
Description: Karuka leaves, from the papaya tree, are widely used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits, including immune support and digestion improvement.
Medicinal Uses:
  • Antimalarial: Traditionally used in the treatment of malaria.
  • Digestive Aid: Helps in improving digestion and treating digestive issues.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Known for its anti-inflammatory properties.
Culinary Uses:
  • Leaf Tea: Used to make herbal teas to improve digestion.
  • Raw Leaves: Added in some cuisines, though often bitter.
Places Where Found:

Karuka (Papaya) trees are native to tropical regions of Central America but are now grown widely in tropical and subtropical regions globally.

Cultural and Historical Significance:

Karuka (Papaya) leaves have been used in traditional medicine across cultures for their wide range of health benefits.

Chemical Composition:
  • Papain: An enzyme with digestive benefits.
  • Flavonoids: Known for antioxidant properties.
  • Alkaloids: Have anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects.
How to Use:
  • Leaf Extract: Can be used in the form of juice or supplements for digestive health.
  • Leaf Tea: Brewed as a tea to aid digestion and reduce inflammation.
Safety and Side Effects:

Karuka leaves are generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, excessive consumption may cause stomach irritation. Pregnant women should consult a doctor before use.
